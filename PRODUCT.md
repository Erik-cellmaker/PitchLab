# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Pre-med undergraduates (typically sophomores and juniors) at US universities who want research experience for their medical school applications. They're stressed, time-constrained, and intimidated by cold emailing professors — they know they should do it but keep putting it off. They are skeptical: they've heard AI tools produce spam. Their core conversion barrier is believing it actually works, not just that it's fast.

## Product Purpose

PitchLab automates personalized professor cold-email campaigns for pre-med students. It looks up each professor's latest published research via PubMed, writes a unique 150-word email referencing that research, and sends it directly from the student's Gmail — all within minutes of ordering. One-time payment, no subscription. Success looks like: students get replies from professors and land research positions.

## Brand Personality

Confident, efficient, student-built. Peers talking to peers. Direct, no fluff, built by someone who understands the struggle. Not a faceless SaaS company — more like a smart friend who already cracked the problem and built a tool for it. The voice is plain-spoken and evidence-forward: every claim backed by specifics (actual school names, actual timelines, actual lab placements).

## Anti-references

- Generic AI-tool landing pages: cream/sand backgrounds, gradient text, hero-metric templates, numbered eyebrows on every section header
- Shady bulk-email services: mass blast aesthetics, anything that looks like Mailchimp for cold spam
- Over-polished VC-startup aesthetic: doesn't try to look like a $50M Series B product; should feel scrappy and real
- Edtech / tutoring platforms: not Chegg, not Khan Academy, not any "learning platform" aesthetic

## Design Principles

1. **Show, don't tell.** The demo email in the hero is the whole pitch. Let the output speak before the copy does.
2. **Evidence over claims.** Real testimonials with real outcomes (specific schools, actual labs) beat vague social proof. Stats are specific (5 min, 200 max, 35+ universities, 0 templates).
3. **Peer voice, not sales voice.** Copy reads like a smart student explaining the tool to a classmate — direct, no startup-marketing filler.
4. **Dark and precise.** The dark theme isn't aesthetic decoration; it signals "tool" over "marketing site" and stands out from the beige-and-blue edtech crowd.
5. **One path, no distractions.** Everything on the page funnels to "Start your campaign." No feature sprawl, no secondary navigation, no dilution.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA target. All body text ≥4.5:1 contrast against its background. Large text (≥18px or bold ≥14px) ≥3:1. Keyboard navigable. Reduced motion respected. Touch targets ≥44×44px.
